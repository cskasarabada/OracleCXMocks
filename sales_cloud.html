10-Stage Sales Journey

Use these narrative-ready callouts while moving across Lead, Opportunity, Quote, and Pursuit demos.

01

Relationship Development

Capture informal history, document influencers, and build trust with stakeholders.

02

Qualification & Requirements Intake

Confirm project needs, validate expectations, and align budget constraints.

03

Concept & Feasibility Review

Engage SMEs to vet capacity assumptions and early designs.

04

Scope Definition

Define camp size requirements, infrastructure needs, and delivery logistics.

05

Internal Review & Pre-Bid Approval

Cross-functional review of models, risk mitigation, legal compliance, and margin targets.

06

Proposal Development

Create engineering drawings, pricing models, and commercial milestones.

07

Client Review & Iterations

Incorporate customer feedback, manage revisions, and sharpen value statements.

08

Negotiation & Alignment

Finalize commercial terms, mitigate risks, and agree on contracting path.

09

Award & Execution

Convert the opportunity, execute contracts, and initiate Fusion Projects handoff.

10

Final Disposition

Record win/loss outcomes, capture competitive insights, and push learnings to analytics.

Gate Criteria – Quality Checkpoints

Each stage requires specific gate criteria before progression, minimizing risk and increasing forecast accuracy.

Early-Stage Gates

  • Customer interest validated and relationship strengthened.
  • Budget & timeline confirmed during Qualification.
  • SME review completed for Feasibility.
  • Ops / engineering checkpoint complete for Scope Definition.

Mid-Stage Gates

  • Finance + Ops approval finalized prior to Pre-Bid.
  • Proposal submitted with disposition plan.
  • Customer feedback received during Client Review.

Late-Stage Gates

  • Commercial alignment confirmed in Negotiation.
  • Contract finalized for Award/Contracting.
  • Disposition confirmed (Closed Won or Closed Lost).
